2020 VT4

Synopsis

One of the closest asteroids to our planet, 2020 VT4, was discovered in November. This is essential for making hazard evaluations and informing the public about the nature and path of this object.

Finding out

Date of Information Encounter: 15th November, 2020
Body Face: The asteroid was discovered by the Mount Lemmon Survey, which is part of a network that tracks near-Earth objects.

Physical attributes

Size: Approx. diameter between 5 to 10m (15-33ft.). It is tiny, smaller than a boulder and large elliptical spaceship.
Make-up: Material: It is most likely made of rock and metal, as is the case with many tiny asteroids, despite the lack of precise compositional data.

The Orbital Dynamics

Classification: 2020 VT4 is an Apollo-type asteroid.
Orbital Period: 1.3 months (EARTH DAYS)
Speed Approach Speed: 2020 VT4 was moving closer at about 8.4 km/s, or around 18,800 mph, which prevails for items of this dimension as well as course.

Closest Approach

On November 13, 2020, the distance was about 400 km, or about 248 miles, to pass close to the surface of the Earth. It is one of the closest encounters by a near-Earth object that has been observed because of its extraordinary closeness.

Impact Risk and Potential Danger Assessment

2020 VT4 did not present any immediate threat when it approached. Its course was perfectly calculated, and there was no chance of an Earth-impact. NASA and other space organizations keep a close eye on NEOs like 2020 VT4 in order to identify any possible risks.
