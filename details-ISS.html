International Space Station (ISS)

Overview

The International Space Station (ISS) is a low Earth orbit satellite used for research. It was launched in 1998, and has been continuously occupied since November 2000.

Key Details

Launch Date: November 20, 1998
Orbit Altitude: 400 km (248 miles) above Earth
Speed: Travels at around 28,000 km/h (17,500 mph)
Orbit Period: Circles Earth every 90 minutes
Dimensions: The ISS is approximately the size of a football field (109 meters).

Structure & Composition

The ISS is made of pressurized modules, solar arrays, and robotic arms. It consists of components launched and assembled in space from the U.S., Russia, Japan, Europe, and Canada.

Function & Purpose

The ISS is a laboratory for scientific research, supporting studies in physics, biology, and astronomy. It helps scientists test new technologies and prepare for missions to Mars and beyond.

Notable Features

The ISS is easily visible from Earth without a telescope. It hosts a rotating crew of astronauts from different space agencies, who live aboard for several months at a time.
