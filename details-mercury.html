Mercury

General Information

Kind: Planetary Earth
Diameter: around 4,880 kilometers (3,032 miles)
Surface Temperature: Variable from 427°C (800°F) during the day to -173°C (-280°F) at night

Orbit Details

Distance from the Sun: About 57.91 million km (36 million miles)
Period of Orbit: 88 Earth days
Period of Rotation: 59 Earth days
Axial Tilt: 0.034 degrees (nearly no tilt)

Speed and Closeness

Orbital Velocity: Approximate average of 47.87 km/s (29.74 mi/s)
Escape Velocity: 4.25 km/s (2.64 miles per hour)
Closest Approach to Earth: Approximately 77 million kilometers (48 million miles) every 116 days

Possible Hazards

Impact Hazard: No direct threat from Mercury to Earth; classified as a near-Earth object (NEO)
Solar Radiation: High solar radiation exposure, important for mission planning
Surface Features: Extensively cratered surface, similar to the Moon
Magnetic Field: Weak magnetic field (about 1% of Earth's)
No Atmosphere: Extremely thin atmosphere, unable to retain heat

Fascinating Information

Day vs. Year: A rotation takes 59 Earth days, while a revolution around the Sun takes 88 days.
Exploration: Only visited by MESSENGER and Mariner 10 spacecraft.
Namesake: Named after the Roman messenger deity.
