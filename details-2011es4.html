2011 ES4

Synopsis

The near-Earth asteroid 2011 ES4 attracted interest because of how close it was to Earth. It is essential to comprehend its characteristics and path in order to evaluate possible hazards and for instructional purposes.

Finding out

Finding out Date: March 7, 2011
Finding Body: The Catalina Sky Survey, a program devoted to monitoring near-Earth objects, made the discovery of the asteroid.

Physical attributes

Dimensions: Approximate diameter: 20 to 50 meters, or 65 to 164 feet.
Make-up: Material: 2011 ES4 is most likely made of rock and metal.

Orbital Dynamics

Type: Apollo class
Orbital Period: 1.37 Earth years.

Approaching Velocity

Velocity: 7.6 km/s (approximately 17,000 mph).

Closest Approach

Date: September 1, 2020
Nearest Distance: 1,830 km (1,140 miles).

Potential Danger Assessment

2011 ES4 did not present any immediate threat. Its course was perfectly calculated.

Implications for Science and Education

Close encounters such as that of 2011 ES4 offer a great opportunity to spread awareness through education on the importance of monitoring NEOs.
