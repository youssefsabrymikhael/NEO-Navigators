What are Near-Earth Objects (NEOs)? Near-Earth Objects (NEOs) are asteroids and comets that have orbits bringing them into proximity with Earth. Monitoring these objects is crucial to identify potential threats, as their trajectories can change due to gravitational influences or collisions with other celestial bodies.
Why is monitoring NEOs important? Regular observation and tracking of NEOs enable astronomers to predict their paths accurately, which is essential for planetary defense strategies aimed at mitigating the risks associated with these celestial bodies.

NEOs vary in size, composition, and origin. Some are remnants from the early solar system, while others are fragments from larger bodies that have broken apart. Understanding these objects helps scientists assess any potential risk they may pose to our planet.
